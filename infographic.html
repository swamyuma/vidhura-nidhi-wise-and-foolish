Vidura Nīti

Wisdom vs. Folly
A visual executive summary of Vidura’s counsel to King Dhṛtarāṣṭra (Mahābhārata, Udyoga Parva), contrasting the defining qualities of the wise (Paṇḍita) and the foolish (Mūḍha).

Core Qualities of the Wise (Paṇḍita)

  • Unwavering Dharma: Never abandons righteousness for personal gain.
  • Emotional Equanimity: Unshaken by praise, blame, joy, or anger.
  • Disciplined Action: Plans carefully and completes what is begun.
  • Resilient Resolve: Acts steadily amid comfort, fear, success, or adversity.
  • Measured Desire: Subordinates fleeting pleasures to lasting good.

Behavioral Hallmarks

  • Discretion: Keeps counsel and plans hidden until execution.
  • Deep Listening: Learns patiently and speaks only when asked.
  • Focused Effort: Treats no duty as trivial; applies full effort.
  • Inner Balance: Does not crave the unattainable or mourn the lost.
  • Self-Mastery: Controls time, impulses, and personal ambition.

Core Traits of the Foolish (Mūḍha)

  • Arrogant Ignorance: Lacks knowledge yet overflows with pride.
  • Misplaced Priorities: Neglects own duties to meddle in others’ affairs.
  • Poor Judgment: Desires the unfit and rejects the appropriate.
  • Erroneous Alliances: Treats enemies as friends and despises well-wishers.
  • Impulsive Anger: Rages despite lacking strength or strategy.

Patterns of Self-Harm

  • Reckless Speech: Talks excessively, even when uninvited.
  • Blind Trust: Relies on the untrustworthy and flatters the unworthy.
  • Misguided Ambition: Opposes those far stronger; chases the impossible.
  • Neglected Duties: Fails in piety, family responsibility, and protection.
  • Hypocrisy: Ignores personal faults while condemning others.
